Guta Demisu Kebede is a mathematician, lecturer, and researcher at the Department of Mathematics, College of Natural and Computational Sciences, Dilla University, Ethiopia. His work sits at the intersection of advanced numerical analysis, differential equations, and accessible mathematics education.

With a deep commitment to mathematical rigor, Guta has contributed to peer-reviewed research published in leading international journals including Wiley, Frontiers in Applied Mathematics & Statistics, BMC Research Notes, and Taylor & Francis — addressing some of the most technically demanding problems in computational mathematics: singularly perturbed time-delay parabolic problems with two small parameters.

Beyond the research lab, Guta is a passionate digital educator. Through his YouTube channel Guta Maths Studio (@GMS_21) and TikTok platform @frewon21, he translates complex mathematical concepts into engaging content accessible to students across Ethiopia and the broader global community.

His vision is simple yet transformative: every student deserves access to world-class mathematics education, regardless of geography or background.

Core Research Domain

Singularly Perturbed Differential Equations

These equations model phenomena where a small parameter multiplies the highest derivative — creating boundary layers and requiring specialized numerical methods. Guta's research advances parameter-uniform convergent schemes for these challenging problems.

Two-Parameter Problem
ε·u''(x,t) − γ·u'(x,t) − u(x,t−τ) = f(x,t)
Core equation type studied: parabolic PDE with two small parameters ε, γ and time delay τ.
Implicit Euler Scheme
U^(n+1) − U^n / Δt ≈ L_h[U^(n+1)]
Time discretization approach used in published numerical schemes for stability.
B-Spline Collocation
u(x) ≈ ∑ cⱼ B³ⱼ(x)
Third-degree B-spline basis functions for high-accuracy spatial discretization.
Parameter-Uniform Convergence
‖U − u‖ ≤ C · (Δt² + h²)
Key convergence result: error bound independent of small perturbation parameters ε, γ.
Richardson Extrapolation
u*= (4U_{2h} − U_h) / 3
Accuracy enhancement technique applied to boost convergence order.
Stability Condition
|λ(A_h)| ≤ 1 ∀ ε,γ ∈ (0,1]
Parameter-independent spectral radius condition guaranteeing numerical stability.
